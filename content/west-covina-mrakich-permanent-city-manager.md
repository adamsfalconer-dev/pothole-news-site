> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents June 2026 events.*

West Covina has a permanent city manager again. On June 2, the City Council took the "acting" off Milan Mrakich's title, handing the person who has run the city's day-to-day operations since May 2025 the job for good.

Mrakich is not a new face at City Hall. He joined West Covina in 2019 as its director of public safety and brings roughly four decades of municipal experience to the permanent post.

**Why it matters:** The city manager is the most powerful unelected official in a California city — the person who writes the budget, hires and fires department heads, and carries out what the council decides. West Covina had run that office on an interim basis for more than a year. The vote ends that limbo and hands one person clear authority over the city's staff and finances.

The appointment was not unanimous. California City News, in reporting by Brittany Maldonado, put the council vote at 4–1. Our sources do not identify who cast the dissenting vote.

One thing the announcement left open: money. Neither the city nor the trade-press coverage disclosed Mrakich's salary or the terms of his employment agreement — figures that, for a permanent city manager, are public and typically surface in the contract the council approves.
