**Previously:** On Saturday we reported that City of Industry sold — or didn't sell — public land on July 9, and that no record says which. Today the hole got measured.

A file-by-file read of Industry's own meeting portal shows the last posted council minutes are for a January 15 special meeting. Nothing since: not February 12 (the Marici battery-plant package), not February 26, not the string of meetings through July 9 (the Workman Mill land sale to the Sanitation Districts). The gap now runs February 12 to the present — five months. The city's affiliated authorities stopped posting minutes in mid-February.

**Why it matters:** Industry's council governs land, bonds and contracts worth far more than its roughly 113 voters could ever watch in person, and it posts no meeting video. Minutes are the only public record of what its five members actually did. For five months, that record is empty. This is a condition, not an accusation — the city may hold draft minutes awaiting approval — but the public-facing record today shows no outcome for any council action taken since mid-February.

Three specific unknowns now sit inside the gap: whether the council approved the Marici 400 MW battery package on February 12; whether it adopted Resolutions CC 2026-22 and 2026-23 selling the Workman Mill parcel on July 9; and the outcome of every consent and contract item between.

One more portal fact: as of this morning, Industry has **zero** future meetings posted — no July 23 council event, no July 16 hydrogen-authority event. The 72-hour posting window for a July 23 meeting opens July 20; we flag it if the calendar is still empty on July 21.
