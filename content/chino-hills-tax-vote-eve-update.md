**Previously:** As we reported Saturday, the Chino Hills City Council is set to decide Tuesday, July 14 — Item 19 on the posted agenda — whether to place a 1-cent sales tax on the November 3 ballot, the city's answer to a budget it says no longer balances.

The new development is what's scheduled for two days *after* that vote: City Manager Rod Hill presents the FY2026-27 budget to the Parks and Recreation Commission on Thursday evening. That makes at least three public budget presentations since June, now extending past the ballot decision itself.

**Why it matters:** The city is putting its budget math in front of every audience it has. If the council votes yes Tuesday, that math becomes the case for the measure between now and November; if it votes no, it becomes the case for whatever gets cut. Either way, Thursday's session is where residents can hear the numbers with a shorter line to the microphone than a council night.

Meanwhile, the commission calendar keeps clearing. Two more July cancellation notices are posted — the Planning Commission's July 7 meeting and the Public Works Commission's July 1. No reasons are stated, and two cancellations are just two cancellations; we note the July pattern without a conclusion and will watch whether Planning's July 21 meeting proceeds.
