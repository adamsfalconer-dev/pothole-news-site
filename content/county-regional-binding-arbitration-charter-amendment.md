The Los Angeles County Board of Supervisors is set to vote Tuesday on whether to send voters a charter amendment that would bar strikes by specified public-safety employees — and require that their labor disputes be settled by impartial binding arbitration.

The measure, carried on the board's supplemental agenda as item 34-A by Supervisor Lindsey Horvath, covers certified employee organizations for firefighters, sheriff's personnel, district attorney investigators, medical examiners and lifeguards. If the board adopts it Tuesday, it heads toward the countywide November 3 ballot.

**Why it matters:** Voters in Pomona, Diamond Bar, Walnut, Rowland Heights and the City of Industry would all vote on this — and three of those places are policed by the very department whose labor disputes it would restructure. Diamond Bar and Walnut buy their policing from LASD by contract, and Rowland Heights is served by the sheriff as unincorporated territory; what an arbitrator someday awards deputies is not an abstraction there, it's an input to the bill. The trade embedded in the measure is a classic one — no strikes, in exchange for a neutral third party setting the terms when bargaining fails — and it would be written into the county charter, not a contract.
