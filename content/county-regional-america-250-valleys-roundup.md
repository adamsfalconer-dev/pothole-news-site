> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents early-July 2026 events.*

The nation's 250th birthday did not arrive the same way in any two places. Over July 3 and 4, communities across the valleys marked the semiquincentennial in their own registers — sacred and civic, high-tech and deeply traditional.

In Chino Hills, the celebration was a light-and-sound show. The BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir, at 15100 Fairfield Ranch Road, hosted "Desi Roots, American Dreams," a free nightly program on July 3 and 4 that tied the country's 250th to California's 175th and to the contributions of the Indian diaspora. Rep. Norma Torres and Chino Hills Mayor Brian Johsz were among those who attended.

In South Pasadena, the weekend was built around a parade. Themed "From Sea to Shining Sea," the city held an 11 a.m. procession on July 4, capped by a 9 p.m. fireworks show at the South Pasadena High School stadium.

In Sierra Madre, an old tradition came back. The city's Independence Day Parade — an annual fixture dating to the mid-1960s — returned July 4 at 10 a.m., with Colleen McKernan and Kevin Drolet serving as grand marshals.

And in San Dimas, the celebration looked to the sky. The downtown "Stars, Stripes and Drone Lights" festival closed July 3 with a nighttime drone show — a festival, not a parade.

Together they are a small map of how the footprint throws a party: a mandir and a high school stadium, a 1960s parade route and a drone swarm, all pointed at the same anniversary.
