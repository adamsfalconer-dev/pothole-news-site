For weeks, a Rowland Heights-led coalition has organized against "AI data centers in the City of Industry" — and until today, neither side of that fight had shown a primary public record. Now we have one.

Industry's February 12 council agenda, pulled from the city's own portal, packaged item 8.2: the **Marici Battery Energy Storage System** — a 400-megawatt facility of 480 Sungrow lithium-ion battery containers across five parcels at 16207–16253 Gale Avenue, interconnecting through Southern California Edison's Walnut Substation. The applicant is Marici Project LLC.

**Why it matters:** This is the biggest energy project in the footprint's paper record, sited on Industry's border with the neighborhoods organizing against it — and the packet shows the city didn't just take up one project. Ordinance 841 would add a new battery-storage chapter to the zoning code **citywide**, a door that stays open long after Marici.

The environmental path is the opposition's core grievance, and the packet confirms its shape: a Mitigated Negative Declaration rather than a full environmental impact report, with a 20-day public comment window that ran December 16 to January 5 — spanning the winter holidays. The package also included a development plan, a conditional use permit, and a Community Benefit Agreement whose fee amount does not appear in the packet text we could extract.

What the record does **not** show: the vote. Industry has posted no minutes for February 12 — or any council meeting since — so whether the package passed is officially unrecorded. Press accounts describe a unanimous approval; that stands unconfirmed until minutes exist. Also unfound: any actual data-center application. The coalition claims "three data centers" are coming; the only "data center" strings in this 6,633-page packet sit in consultant résumés. We'll keep looking, and we'll say so either way.

One timeline note from the same agenda: the council met in closed session that day to negotiate over the Workman Mill-area parcel it moved to sell to the Sanitation Districts on July 9 — that deal was in the works by mid-February.

**Previously:** The RHCCC passed a unanimous resolution May 11 opposing AI data centers in Industry "or surrounding areas," sent to surrounding cities, agencies and legislators.
