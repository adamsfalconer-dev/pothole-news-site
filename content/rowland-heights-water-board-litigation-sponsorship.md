The elected board that runs Rowland Heights' water meets Tuesday with two items worth a resident's attention: a closed-session briefing on litigation the district is a party to, and a decision on whether to give money to the Rowland Heights Community Coordinating Council.

The litigation: *San Juan Water District et al. v. State Water Resources Control Board*, Sacramento Superior Court case 25WM000238. The agenda states plainly that "Rowland Water District is a party." What the case is about — and which side of it the district sits on — is not stated on the agenda, and we haven't confirmed it elsewhere, so we're not characterizing it. We will before we write about it again.

**Why it matters:** Rowland Heights has no city hall, which makes its elected water board one of the few bodies residents actually vote onto — and this is our first full read of its business. A district in live litigation involving the state's water regulator, whatever the subject, is carrying legal costs and legal risk on ratepayers' behalf. That's worth watching on the merits once the merits are knowable.

The sponsorship item is local texture with a twist: item 2.4 asks the board to consider a sponsorship request from the RHCCC — the same community council that passed May's resolution against AI data centers in neighboring Industry, and which meets Monday night. Two RWD directors, Szu Pei Lu-Yang and John Bellah, serve as the district's liaisons to the RHCCC. That's an overlap worth noting, and nothing more: it is not a conflict claim. The agenda doesn't state a dollar amount; the same consent run includes a Buckboard Days Parade sponsorship.
