On Wednesday, the City of Industry's council was set to decide whether to sell a piece of public land. Two days later, no one outside city hall can say what it decided.

The two resolutions — CC 2026-22, declaring a parcel (APN 8120-027-272) "exempt surplus," and CC 2026-23, selling it to County Sanitation District No. 18 — were on the July 9 agenda. But the city's meeting portal shows the July 9 session with no minutes file and no video. The sale price and terms sit inside a 132-page packet the city has posted but not summarized. The result is a public land transaction with no public outcome.

*Previously:* this is not a one-off. Industry's council has gone months without posting minutes for any meeting — a blackout this newsroom has tracked since early July.

**Why it matters:** When a city sells public land, California's Surplus Land Act sets out a process meant to be visible — priority for affordable housing and parks, disclosure of terms. A city of 264 residents that also controls hundreds of millions in bonds, JPAs, and land is exactly where that visibility matters most. The law describes what should be on the record; Industry's portal shows what is: nothing.

To be precise about what we know and don't: we know the resolutions were agendized and that, as of Friday, the meeting carried no outcome record. We do not know how the council voted, or whether it acted at all. We are not alleging the city broke the law — we are reporting that the public record cannot tell you whether it did.
