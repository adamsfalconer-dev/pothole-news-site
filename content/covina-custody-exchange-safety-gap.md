> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents a June 2026 shooting and a standing infrastructure gap.*

The handoff was supposed to be routine: on the evening of June 9, people met in the parking lot of a Home Depot on West Badillo Street in Covina to exchange a child in a custody arrangement. According to Covina police, an argument between two men escalated, one of them opened fire, and the other died at the scene. One person was arrested.

We are deliberately spare about the rest. The arrested person is presumed innocent. The suspect's identity, any suggestion that children saw what happened, and any claim of a domestic-violence backdrop have circulated from a single, unverified source; none is confirmed, and none is stated here.

**Why it matters:** Strip the case to what is verifiable and a policy question remains: where are families in this part of the valley supposed to safely exchange a child? Custody exchanges are a known flashpoint — two people in conflict, meeting by court necessity — and the safest versions happen at monitored locations. This one happened in a big-box parking lot because, for residents of Covina, that is effectively the default.

Neither Covina nor neighboring West Covina advertises a designated safe-exchange zone. Other agencies in the region do: the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department runs camera-monitored "safe exchange zones" at its stations — explicitly for both online-sale meetups and custody exchanges — and the LAPD and Montebello police offer their own. But Covina is policed by its own department, so a Sheriff's zone a few cities over would not have covered this handoff. The gap here is specifically the city's.

The tools to close it exist. California's Family Code lets a judge order conditions to protect a child during exchanges, and the state funds supervised-exchange and visitation centers through a dedicated grant program. What the east valley appears to lack is the nearest, simplest version: a monitored, well-lit, public place a parent can be pointed to instead of a store parking lot.
