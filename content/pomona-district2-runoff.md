Pomona's District 2 council seat is going to a November runoff. No candidate won a majority in the June 2 primary, so the top two finishers — incumbent Victor Preciado and challenger Jacky Elizalde — will meet again on the Nov. 3 ballot.

In the Los Angeles County registrar's official results, Preciado led the three-way field with 1,154 votes, or 45.38 percent — 118 short of the majority a candidate needs to take a Pomona council seat outright. Elizalde drew 840 votes, 33.03 percent, and Samantha Zavala-Angulo 549, or 21.59 percent. Zavala-Angulo, who finished third, is out; Preciado and Elizalde carry forward.

Pomona's charter runs its council races in two steps. A candidate who wins more than half the vote in the June primary is elected on the spot, and no November election is held for that seat. If no one clears that bar, the charter says, the two highest finishers "shall be the candidates and the only candidates" whose names appear on the November ballot. Preciado's 45 percent led the field but left him short of the line, so District 2 goes to the runoff.

It is the only one of Pomona's three contested seats that voters will still decide this fall. In District 3, Nora Garcia won outright in June with 71.87 percent. In District 5, Yvonne Cobarrubias won the closest of the three council races — 2,458 votes to Elliott Rothman's 2,446, a margin of 12 — and her 50.12 percent just cleared the majority that spared her a runoff. Both seats are settled.

The runoff gives District 2 voters a second, narrower choice, and it sends an incumbent who won a plurality but not a majority back to voters. The June round was small: 2,543 ballots decided it, and Preciado finished 314 votes ahead of Elizalde — a gap a low-turnout fall runoff can close or widen.
