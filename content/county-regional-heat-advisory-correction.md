Los Angeles County has a **heat advisory** in effect for its Inland Coast zone — which covers the Pomona, Diamond Bar, Walnut and Rowland Heights valleys — running **Wednesday, July 8 through Tuesday, July 14**, according to the county's own July 8 notice. A separate advisory covers the Santa Clarita Valley on nearly the same dates.

That matters because it corrects the record. This newsroom's Sunday brief carried a secondhand press claim of an "Extreme Heat Watch" for **July 14–16**. The county's primary release says something narrower on both counts: the designation is an *advisory* — a lower National Weather Service tier than a watch or warning — and the window closes **Tuesday**, not the weekend. No 7/15–16 extension appears in the county record as of today.

Why it's worth getting right: an advisory still means real heat and real risk for anyone without air conditioning, but the label sets the response, and over-stating it erodes trust as much as under-stating it. The county points residents to free **cooling centers, splash pads and public pools**, listed at ready.lacounty.gov/heat or by calling **211**; LA library branches also serve as cool spaces during business hours.

One caveat we're keeping honest: the LA County release does **not** cover Chino Hills, which sits in San Bernardino County. Whether the SB County side is under its own advisory is a separate check we haven't completed.
