Azusa property owners and the tenants who pay their trash bills have until the close of Monday night's public hearing to protest a rate increase that would set the single-family cart rate at $49.07 a month starting with bills issued on or after Aug. 1 — and allow annual increases for the five years after that with no further public hearing.

The City Council takes up Resolution 2026-C42 at its 7:30 p.m. meeting Monday, July 20, at the Azusa Auditorium, 213 E. Foothill Blvd. The resolution would raise collection rates for Athens Services, the city's franchise hauler, by 4.97 percent — matching a federal price index for garbage collection over the year ending this January, according to the notice the city mailed to ratepayers. Seniors would pay $44.17 a month. The same schedule sets multifamily cart rates at the single-family levels and puts the standard once-a-week 3-yard commercial bin service — trash, recycling and organics together — at $462.33 a month.

The larger change is what follows. From July 2027 through July 2031, the notice authorizes the city to adjust rates each year "automatically without a further public hearing" — by that index plus one percentage point, with any increase at least 3 percent and no more than 10 percent in a fiscal year. Ratepayers would get at least 30 days' mailed notice of each one.

Monday is the moment to object, and the rules are specific. Any record owner of a parcel — or a tenant directly responsible for the trash bill — may file a written protest, one per parcel. It must be on paper, with a printed and signed name, street address, utility account number and a statement of opposition; the notice says "oral, telephonic, or e-mailed protests will not be counted" as a written protest. A protest counts only if the city clerk has it in hand — not postmarked — before the hearing closes: by mail or hand delivery to 213 E. Foothill Blvd. with the envelope marked "2026 Trash & Organic Rates Adjustment Public Hearing," or handed to the clerk at the meeting. If owners of a majority of parcels — 50 percent plus one — file protests, the rates cannot be imposed, according to the notice.

The agenda also allows emailed comments of up to 250 words, received by 5 p.m. the day of the meeting, into the record — those are not protests — and residents can speak remotely by Microsoft Teams or phone.

Last year's increase, effective July 2025, was 4.80 percent, according to Athens' customer letter.
