Los Angeles County supervisors are scheduled to vote Tuesday, July 21, on creating a $1 million Altadena Soil Remediation Program, with the county's development authority running the work.

Three related items make up the package. Item 13 on the board's agenda would authorize a funding agreement with the Los Angeles County Development Authority "for an amount not to exceed $1,000,000" — a cap that includes contingency and the authority's administrative costs — inside the same item's larger $5 million appropriation of Climate Equity Account money to various county budget units. Two companion items on the development authority's own agenda — its board meets the same morning — would designate the authority to "act as the agent of the County of Los Angeles" for the program and formally establish it.

The items say how the work would be delivered — remediation projects handled through a board-approved job order contract — and how it would be tracked: the authority would have to report every six months on money used and work completed until the fund is spent. They do not say why Altadena's soil needs remediating: no cause and no contaminant is named in any of the three items. Nor do they say who would qualify, or how many properties $1 million covers.

Item 13 sits on the consent calendar, where "all matters are approved by one motion unless held" — meaning the soil money gets no separate vote Tuesday unless the item is held.

Residents who want to address the board can attend at 9:30 a.m. in Board Hearing Room 381B at the Kenneth Hahn Hall of Administration, 500 W. Temple St. in Los Angeles; call (213) 306-3065 with access code 2538 037 9804 and password 2672026, then press *3 when the item is called; or submit written comment at publiccomment.bos.lacounty.gov.
