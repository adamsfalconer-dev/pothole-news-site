> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents July 2026 events.*

Temple City now has two seats at the table where Southern California works out how it will move people and power its future. The San Gabriel Valley Council of Governments has appointed two of the city's elected officials to policy committees of the Southern California Association of Governments, the regional body known as SCAG.

Councilwoman Cynthia Sternquist takes a seat on SCAG's Transportation Committee. Mayor Pro Tem Ed Chen joins its Energy & Environment Committee. Both now appear on the agency's committee rosters.

**Why it matters:** SCAG is the agency that coordinates transportation and environmental planning across the Southern California region, and its policy committees are where those priorities get shaped before they reach the full board. For a small San Gabriel Valley city, a seat on each is a chance to punch above its weight — a voice in regional decisions that are otherwise driven by the region's largest cities and counties.

It is worth being precise about what these seats are. They are assignments to SCAG's policy committees — the panels that steer transportation and energy-and-environment policy — not officer positions on the SGVCOG's own governing board. The distinction matters because "regional council seat" can suggest a leadership post at the council of governments; what Sternquist and Chen hold are committee seats at SCAG.

The appointments were reported July 7 by heysocal and the Arcadia Weekly. The SGVCOG — the alliance of San Gabriel Valley cities that holds seats on SCAG — distributes those seats among its member cities, and two going to Temple City gives one small city an unusual amount of regional reach.
