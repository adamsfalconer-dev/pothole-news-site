Diamond Bar changed the person who runs its day-to-day government, and it happened with the kind of quiet that marks a planned succession. The City Council voted 5-0 on June 16 to appoint Assistant City Manager Ryan McLean as the city's next city manager. He took over July 1, succeeding Dan Fox, who retired after nine years running city operations.

**Why it matters:** In a contract city like Diamond Bar — where the sheriff, fire, and much of the engineering work run through Los Angeles County and outside vendors — the city manager is the hinge. That person sets the agenda, manages the county contracts, and is the continuity most residents never see. A unanimous internal promotion signals stability, not upheaval; it also means the official who will steer Diamond Bar through this fall's election and next year's budget is new to the top job.

This is a change we owe readers plainly: a footprint city replaced its chief executive, on the record, by unanimous vote. There is no dispute in the file — a long-tenured manager retired and his deputy stepped up.
