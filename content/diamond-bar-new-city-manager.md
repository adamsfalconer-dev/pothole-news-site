Ryan McLean became Diamond Bar's city manager on July 1, taking over from Dan Fox, who the city says led its daily operations for the past nine years.

The council approved McLean's employment agreement at its June 16 meeting. It sets a base salary of $289,806.03 a year, effective July 1 — a floor rather than a flat figure, since the city agrees to adjust it "with the same Cost of Living or Consumer Price Index adjustment as is provided to Executive Management employees." The agreement adds a car allowance of $750 a month and $15,000 a year in deferred compensation.

McLean is employed "in an 'at-will' capacity serving at the pleasure of the City Council," the agreement says. If the council fires him without cause while he is "still willing and able to perform the duties of City Manager," the city owes him a lump sum equal to "nine (9) month's aggregate salary and benefits" — at his base salary alone, roughly $217,000; the nine months also covers benefits. The contract treats a pay cut, or a resignation the council majority asks for, as a termination. It also bars the council from giving him notice within 90 days after an election that seats a new member.

McLean was already Diamond Bar's assistant city manager. The council selected him, and Mayor Steve Tye, Mayor Pro Tem Ruth M. Low and the city attorney negotiated the agreement that returned for the June 16 vote, according to the staff report.

The city says the council approved it unanimously, 5-0; the minutes, which Diamond Bar posts inside a later meeting's packet, are not out yet.
