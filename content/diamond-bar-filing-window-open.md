Diamond Bar's candidate filing window for the November 3 election **opened at 7:30 a.m. today** and runs through **5 p.m. Friday, August 7**, covering council **Districts 1, 3 and 4**, according to the city's election page. If an incumbent in any of those districts doesn't file, that district's window extends to August 12.

The field on record hasn't changed since the weekend: **District 1 is already contested** — incumbent **Steve Tye** against challenger **Ruben Torres** — while **Andrew Chou** (D3) and **Lee Mao** (D4) are the names on file so far. Those come from Form 501 intention statements, which signal a run but don't qualify anyone; nomination papers still have to be filed and verified.

Here's the accountability wrinkle worth flagging on day one: Diamond Bar takes those **Form 501s by email to the city clerk**, not through a public e-filing portal. So unlike a county-style live filer list, there's no public feed of who's jumping in — the field only becomes visible when the clerk's page updates or when a candidate goes public. For a watchdog trying to track a contested seat as it forms, that's a blind spot, not a scandal, but it shapes how fast anyone outside city hall learns who's running.

Our daily filer watch is running against that constraint. We also have a related open thread: Diamond Bar's own Planning Commission agenda cleared its 72-hour online-posting mark late twice in the past week, and the next commission meeting is Tuesday.
