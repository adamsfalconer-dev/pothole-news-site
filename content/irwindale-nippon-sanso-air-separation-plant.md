> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents June 2026 events.*

Irwindale has a new piece of industrial infrastructure, and it makes something every factory floor eventually needs: air, taken apart. Nippon Sanso Matheson said it has started production at an air-separation unit in the city — a plant that pulls oxygen, nitrogen and argon out of the atmosphere for sale to manufacturers.

The company announced the startup in a release dated June 17, carried on the business wires June 22. The Irwindale unit joins an existing Nippon Sanso Matheson plant in Vernon, giving the company two production points in the Southern California industrial belt.

**Why it matters:** A merchant air-separation plant is a supplier to suppliers. Its oxygen, nitrogen and argon feed aerospace, electronics, food and beverage, health care and metal fabrication — the industries that fill the warehouses and yards of cities like Irwindale. For an industrial city, a plant like this is less a marquee employer than a link in the chain that keeps other manufacturers running, and a fixed piece of the local industrial base.

How big a piece is the open question. The company called the project "a significant investment" but disclosed no dollar figure, no production capacity and no job count — the numbers that would show how large a footprint it will carry in the city. The startup was announced by Chairman and Chief Executive Alan Draper and Rick Neiner, the company's vice president for Bulk-West.
