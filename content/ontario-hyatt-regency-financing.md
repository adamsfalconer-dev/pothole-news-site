> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents June 2026 events.*

A hotel that went to a bankruptcy auction two years ago is getting a nine-figure second life. On June 16, the developer National CORE said it had secured roughly $103.5 million in tax-exempt bond financing to redevelop the former Ontario Airport Hotel into a 295-room Hyatt Regency, less than a mile from Ontario International Airport.

The property, at 700 N. Haven Avenue, was acquired by National CORE through a bankruptcy auction in August 2024. The financing clears the way for a full renovation, with the hotel targeted to reopen under the Hyatt Regency flag in early 2027.

**Why it matters:** Ontario's airport is one of the region's growth stories, and the hotels around it are part of the economic engine that growth feeds. Returning a nearly 300-room, full-service hotel to service near the terminals means construction work now, permanent hospitality jobs later, and — for the city — a revived room-tax base on a site that had gone dark.

The exact shape of the deal depends on who's describing it. National CORE's own announcement broke the financing into about $74.5 million in tax-exempt revenue bonds plus roughly $27.3 million in PACE — property-assessed clean energy — financing. The adviser JLL, in an account reported by Hotel Management, cited $77.1 million in bonds and $26.5 million in C-PACE. The two descriptions don't reconcile to the dollar; both land near $103.5 million, which is the figure we use.

The project team carries national names: Hyatt Regency as the flag, Manhattan Hospitality Advisors as operator, Gensler as architect, and JLL as the financing adviser.
