> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents June 2026 events.*

Baldwin Park High School will keep the "Braves" nickname it has carried for more than 70 years — even as a new California law that took effect July 1, 2026 lists "Braves" among the Native American mascot names it prohibits. The district's route to keeping the name runs through the law itself.

The law is AB 3074, an expansion of California's Racial Mascots Act, signed by Gov. Gavin Newsom on Sept. 27, 2024. It bars public schools from using "Braves" and similar terms — but it leaves one door open. Under Education Code §221.3(e), a school may keep such a name if it obtains written consent from a local federally recognized tribe. On June 30, Baldwin Park Unified announced it had done exactly that, securing an official letter of consent from the Pala Band of Mission Indians.

It is a case study in how a decades-old identity survives a new prohibition — not by fighting the state, but by using the exemption the Legislature wrote into the ban. For Baldwin Park, it resolves the fate of a name older than most of the people who cheer it.

The arrangement is broader than a signature on a letter. The district says the partnership with the Pala Band also includes a review of the school's imagery and branding, the teaching of Native American history in the curriculum, cultural events, and ongoing engagement between students and the tribe. Superintendent Dr. Froilan N. Mendoza and Principal Deborah Madrigal lead the district and campus behind the arrangement. KTLA's Will Conybeare reported the partnership.

One point sits unresolved, and it is not ours to resolve. The statute conditions the exemption on consent from a "local" federally recognized tribe, and the Pala Band of Mission Indians is headquartered in San Diego County, not the San Gabriel Valley. Whether an out-of-area tribe's consent satisfies the law's "local" language is a legal question for others; what the record shows is that Baldwin Park used the written-consent exemption and obtained the Pala Band's letter.
