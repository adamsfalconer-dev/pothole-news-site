> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents the April and May 2026 record.*

The employees in one of La Verne's bargaining units voted out the union that had represented them since 2002 and picked Teamsters Local 1932 instead.

The secret election was held April 7, and it went 38–13 in favor of representation by the Teamsters, decertifying City Employees Associates, according to a staff report by Assistant City Manager JR Ranells in the council's May 4 agenda packet. CEA had been the exclusive representative of the city's miscellaneous employee unit, one of several employee groups the city bargains with, since August 2002. The Teamsters filed their petition for decertification and recognition with the city on January 9.

The council took the result up on May 4 and voted 4-0 to receive it, file it and recognize the Teamsters. No formal council action was required under the employer-employee relations rules the city has adopted, the staff report says; the item came to the council "for Council acknowledgment due to the significance of the transition and the longstanding prior representation."
