Pothole News follows the Society of Professional Journalists' principles — **seek truth and report it, minimize harm, act independently, and be accountable and transparent.** This page says what that means in practice, including how we use AI.

## AI transparency

**Pothole News is produced with the help of AI tools, under human editorial control.** We use software to monitor public meeting agendas, packets, filings, and records across a six-city, two-county footprint far larger than a newsroom our size could otherwise cover, and to draft summaries in plain language.

Our commitments:

- **A human is accountable for every published sentence.** Nothing reaches you without human review.
- **AI drafts; it does not decide what's true.** Every factual claim traces to a public record or a named source, and we keep those sources.
- **We label confidence honestly.** If something is confirmed from a primary document, we treat it as fact. If it's unconfirmed, we say "unconfirmed" or don't publish it. If a fact may be out of date (for example, who holds an office before an election), we date it.
- **We never publish AI-generated claims about a person's alleged wrongdoing** without independent human verification and, where warranted, legal review.

## Translation (Spanish edition) · Traducción (edición en español)

Pomona is a majority-Latino community, and much of the public's business here is never covered in Spanish. We publish a Spanish edition so that coverage reaches the readers it's about.

- **AI-assisted, human-reviewed.** Spanish editions are drafted with AI translation and **reviewed by a fluent human editor before publication.** We do not publish machine translation unread.
- **Register.** We write in clear es-US / es-MX Spanish for a local readership, not a word-for-word mirror of the English.
- **One standard across editions.** Confidence labeling, the never-publish gate, sourcing rules, and this ethics policy apply identically in English and Spanish.
- **Corrections apply across editions.** A correction to a story is made in **every** language that story ran in, logged the same way. See Corrections below. / *Las correcciones se hacen en todos los idiomas en que apareció la nota.*

## Sourcing & verification

- We report from **primary public records** — agendas, packets, minutes, filings, court records, and public meetings — wherever possible.
- We **do not state rumors as fact.** Correlations (a pattern in contracts, a timing coincidence) are leads we keep investigating, not conclusions we publish.
- **Anonymous sources** are a last resort, used only with a senior editor's sign-off and never as the sole basis for an accusation.
- When we rely on another outlet's reporting, we **credit and link it** and quote sparingly (see Attribution).

## Attribution & aggregation

Local journalism is a commons. When another newsroom breaks a story:

- We **credit the outlet and the reporter** at first reference and **link to their work**.
- We **quote no more than two sentences** and never reproduce paywalled text.
- We **acknowledge scoops the same week** ("first reported by…").
- Our weekly **Worth your click** sends readers to the best local coverage we didn't produce.
- We never cite or amplify content mills that impersonate local news.

## Independence & conflicts

Pothole News is editorially independent. We disclose any relationship that could look like a conflict, and staff recuse from stories where they have one. We accept no gifts, no pay-to-play, and no coverage-for-consideration.

## Corrections

**We fix errors openly.** When we get something wrong, we correct it, mark the change, and log it — we do not silently edit. See our **[corrections log](/corrections/)**. To flag an error: **corrections@pothole.news**.

## Legal & records

Public meetings and officials acting in public are subject to reporting and, where lawful, recording. We use the California Public Records Act and follow the legal windows that govern police-video and records disclosures. We route sensitive stories to counsel before publication when the law requires it. We never secretly record a private one-on-one conversation. When you send us a tip, we protect your identity — see [how we handle tips](/tips/).
