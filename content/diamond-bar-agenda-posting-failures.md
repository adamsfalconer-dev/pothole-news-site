Diamond Bar has a pattern worth watching, and it gets its next test in the next few hours. On July 9, the city's Traffic & Transportation Commission met — but no agenda for that meeting was ever published to the city's online portal. Now the Planning Commission's agenda for Tuesday, July 14 faces a state posting deadline that falls tonight, roughly 6:30 p.m., and as of Friday it was still unpublished online.

California's open-meeting law requires a regular meeting's agenda to be posted at least 72 hours in advance. For a Tuesday-evening meeting, that clock runs out Saturday evening.

**Why it matters:** Agendas are how residents know what their government is about to do. When a commission meets with no agenda online, the public loses its window to weigh in — and a second missed deadline in one week would turn a one-off into a habit. In a city that runs lean and contracts out much of its government, the posting portal is often the only real-time view residents have.

An important limit on what we can say: we checked the online portal, not the physical posting board at city hall, and this weekend our tools can't read Diamond Bar's browser-only portal at all — so tonight's status is unverified as we publish. That makes this a records-practice question, not a legal finding. The city may have posted a paper agenda on time. But the online record — the one most residents actually use — is where the gaps keep showing up.
