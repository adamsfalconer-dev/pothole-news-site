San Gabriel property owners — and tenants who pay the trash bill directly — have until just before noon today to get a written protest against the city's proposed new trash rates to City Hall, at 425 S. Mission Dr., by mail or drop-off. After that, they can still hand one to the city clerk in person until tonight's hearing closes. The City Council opens the Proposition 218 hearing at its 6:30 p.m. meeting; if a majority of affected customers file written protests, the council cannot approve the rates.

The hearing covers new Athens Services rates and a new formula for raising them. Under the change, described in the city's mailed notice, residential customers keep their current service and add a free 96-gallon recycling barrel, with no rate change beyond the new formula; commercial and multifamily accounts move to a bundled three-container rate, and those that "simply accept the new barrel will see their rates increase," the notice says. The proposed dollar amounts are on a chart attached to the mailed notice; the copies posted on the city's website don't include it, and questions go to Public Works at (626) 308-2825. For scale, the city's posted schedule, effective July 2025, lists $48.81 a month for single-family curb service with 90-gallon barrels.

The formula is the lasting change. Rates now rise each July with the Los Angeles-area consumer price index; the proposal switches to a national "trash CPI" plus 1 percentage point — the notice's own example turns a 2.8% index reading into a 3.8% increase — and future formula increases would take effect without additional protest hearings.

Protests must be written; email and fax don't count. Each needs the parcel number or service address, a statement of opposition, and the name and original signature of a record owner or a tenant directly responsible for the bill — one protest counts per parcel. Mailed or dropped-off protests must reach the city before 12 p.m. today — received, not postmarked; the agenda says the council will also accept written protests at the hearing itself before the clerk certifies the count. If protests fall short of a majority, the council may adopt the rates and formula tonight.

Also tonight, the council takes up resolutions placing a sales-tax measure on the November ballot. The agenda doesn't state the rate; in June, according to the meeting minutes, the council unanimously directed staff to bring back a measure of up to one cent. The agenda for that June discussion put the city's needs at more than $100 million "to protect services provided to residents and renovate or replace aging infrastructure." San Gabriel's sales-tax rate now stands at 10.5%, per the state tax department's schedule.

To speak tonight: submit a speaker card in the Council Chamber at 425 S. Mission Dr., comment through the city's online form by 4 p.m. (form comments are forwarded to the council, not read aloud), or join by Zoom (webinar ID 981 6519 1661). The meeting streams on the city's YouTube channel. A separate closed session on anticipated litigation begins at 5:30 p.m.
