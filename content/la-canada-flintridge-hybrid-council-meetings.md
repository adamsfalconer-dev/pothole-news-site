> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents July 2026 events.*

La Cañada Flintridge residents no longer have to show up in person to speak at a City Council meeting. Effective July 1, the city announced it will offer hybrid public participation — in person and by Zoom — at council meetings.

The mechanics are simple: remote residents join the Zoom webinar and use its "raise hand" feature to get in the queue for public comment, while people in the room continue to submit speaker cards.

**Why it matters:** For a resident who can't easily get to city hall on a weeknight — because of a disability, a work shift, or caregiving — remote comment is the difference between having a say in a local decision and missing it entirely. The change hands that access to anyone with an internet connection.

The city framed the move as a transparency and accessibility measure, quoting Mayor Kim Bowman, and tied it to Senate Bill 707, a new state law that updates the remote-access rules in California's Ralph M. Brown Act — the open-meetings statute governing how local governments must let the public watch and take part.

One note on how this happened: the city rolled the change out administratively, through an announcement, rather than a discrete council vote we could locate. This was a City Hall decision, not a recorded action of the council.
