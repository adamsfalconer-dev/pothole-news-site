> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account covers a state law that took effect July 1, 2026, and the city's response to it.*

Starting July 1, state law requires the La Cañada Flintridge City Council to let residents comment at its meetings by phone or video, not only in person. The mandate reaches a city of about 20,000 people for an unlikely reason: it sits inside Los Angeles County, whose population clears the threshold the law actually uses.

Senate Bill 707 requires any city council in a county of 600,000 or more residents to offer the public a two-way telephone or video option at its meetings, not merely a livestream to watch. The requirement runs through 2029, before it's repealed on Jan. 1, 2030. The city announced its response on July 1, framing the change as bringing La Cañada Flintridge "into compliance" with the new law and quoting Mayor Kim Bowman on transparency and accessibility.

The city's own record shows more than a press release behind it. Two weeks earlier, on June 16, the council's agenda carried a formal item — a staff report from City Attorney Adrian Guerra and a draft resolution to adopt a policy implementing the law. Guerra's report told the council plainly: "By July 1, 2026, the City Council must provide for hybrid meetings."

Whether the council actually adopted that resolution isn't yet on the public record. Minutes for the June 16 meeting haven't been posted.

Nor has the new format been tested in practice. The council hasn't met since June 16. Its next scheduled meeting, July 7, was canceled; the one after that, July 21, has no agenda posted yet.
