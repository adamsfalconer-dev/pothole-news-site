Five days after the Pomona City Council met, the city still has not confirmed what it decided. All 15 items from the July 6 meeting — including the resolutions certifying June's primary election and calling a November runoff in District 2 — remain in Draft, and the city's own records system says the outcomes are "not available."

The timing is what makes it matter. Pomona's candidate nomination window opens Monday. Anyone deciding whether to run for the District 2 seat is doing so before the city has formally confirmed the runoff that created the opening — and before the primary that set the field is certified on the record.

**Why it matters:** Draft minutes are normal for a few days; a council can't approve a meeting's record until its next meeting. But the gap here overlaps a live election calendar, and it means the single most consequential set of votes a city takes — certifying an election — is unconfirmed at exactly the moment candidates need certainty. This is a records-timeliness question, not an allegation: the votes almost certainly happened as expected. The point is that "almost certainly" is all the public record currently supports.

We are holding the line on the outcomes. Legistar shows the results as unavailable, so we are not reporting tallies we cannot source — including for the four retroactive contract awards on the same agenda, which remain their own open thread.
