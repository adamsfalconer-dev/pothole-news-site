> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents June 2026 events.*

La Cañada Unified School District is getting a new leader, and it did not go far to find one. Wendy Sinnette, who has run the district since 2011 and worked in it for 24 years, is retiring — and her successor is already named. Debra Cradduck, Ed.D., becomes superintendent on Aug. 1, 2026.

For a small, high-achieving district like La Cañada Unified, the superintendent's chair carries outsized weight over budgets, hiring and the tone of its schools. What stands out in this change is its smoothness: no interim leader, no gap, no outside-search drama. The district is handing off from one insider to another.

Sinnette's tenure spans more than two decades. According to the La Cañada Outlook, she joined the district in 2002 as principal of Palm Crest Elementary and became superintendent in 2011. Her departure is a retirement, not a forced exit — she is stepping down after 24 years with the district.

Cradduck is very much a product of the district she will lead. The Outlook reports that she is a graduate of La Cañada High School and has spent eight years as principal of Paradise Canyon Elementary and eight more as the district's assistant superintendent of human resources. The handoff is direct, with no interim superintendent named to bridge the two.

Some formalities live in the district's own records rather than in public view. The board's formal appointment of Cradduck — the vote and its date — and the terms of her contract were not detailed in the announcement, and Sinnette's exact last day has not been pinned down publicly. What is set is the start date: Aug. 1.
