Pomona's District 2 council race is going to November. In June's primary, incumbent Victor Preciado led with 47.06% — 737 votes — but fell short of the majority needed to win outright. Challenger Jacky Elizalde took 35.12% (550 votes) and Samantha Zavala-Angulo 17.82% (279), so the top two advance to a November 3 runoff between Preciado and Elizalde.

The timing lands with the calendar: Pomona's candidate nomination window opens Monday, July 13, giving residents across the city's contested districts their first chance to file to run.

**Why it matters:** A runoff means District 2 voters get a second, sharper choice in the fall, and an incumbent who won a plurality but not a majority heads into it on notice. For anyone weighing a run elsewhere in Pomona, this week is the starting gun.

One honesty note: the June results are not yet confirmed on the city's own record. Pomona's certification of the primary remains in Draft minutes as of this weekend (a separate story), so these figures come from secondary tallies and stand as unconfirmed until the city adopts them.
