At 7:30 a.m. Monday, the clock starts on who actually runs Diamond Bar. The nomination window opens for council Districts 1, 3 and 4 — three of the city's five seats — and closes at 5 p.m. Friday, August 7.

The city clerk's own election page already shows a contest. In District 1, incumbent Steve Tye filed his candidate-intention statement (Form 501) back on January 6; Ruben Torres filed his on May 7. In District 3, incumbent Andrew Chou has filed; in District 4, Lee Mao. A 501 declares intent and unlocks fundraising — it puts nobody on the ballot. Candidates qualify only by filing nomination papers, by appointment with the clerk, and clearing signature verification.

**Why it matters:** Diamond Bar's elections have a way of being settled before voters see them — this is the moment that determines whether November 3 is a real choice or a formality in each district. A contested D1 before the window even opens is the early signal. If any incumbent doesn't file by August 7, that district's window extends to August 12 for challengers.

The money rules to watch: Diamond Bar has no local contribution limit, so the state default of $5,900 per election applies.

One piece of county plumbing rides alongside: the city's request to consolidate its November 3 election with the county ballot (item 26-4178) appears on the LA County Board of Supervisors' draft log for July 21 — a draft agenda, so subject to change.
