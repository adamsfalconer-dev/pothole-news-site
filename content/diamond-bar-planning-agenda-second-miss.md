**Previously:** On Saturday we reported that Diamond Bar's agenda-posting record faced its next test at 6:30 that evening — the 72-hour online deadline for Tuesday's Planning Commission meeting. The deadline came and went.

As of Sunday morning, the city's own meeting portal shows the strange shape of the miss: the system records that an agenda for Tuesday's 6:30 p.m. meeting exists and is marked "Published" — but the list of files the public can actually open is empty. The agenda has been produced. It just hasn't been made visible.

**Why it matters:** An agenda posted 72 hours ahead is the whole deal of the Brown Act's notice rule — it's how a resident finds out a project on their street is up for a vote *before* the vote. And this is the second miss in a week: the Traffic and Transportation Commission's July 9 agenda was never published online at all.

The honest boundary on this story: our proof covers **online** posting only. The law can be satisfied by physical posting at City Hall, and nobody has checked the physical board. This is a documented posting-practice problem on the city's own portal — it is not a finding that Diamond Bar violated the Brown Act, and we aren't claiming one.

A question to the clerk's office about how the agenda was posted is drafted and staged, awaiting the Publisher's go-ahead; no call has been made.
