> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents April 2026 events.*

Walnut is the latest Southern California city to write rules for electric bikes and motorized scooters. On its April 22 agenda, the City Council took up Resolution 26-25, establishing fines for violations involving e-bikes, motorized bicycles and other motorized devices — a measure the city paired with a new regulatory ordinance it has since announced publicly as its "New Electric Bike & Motorized Scooter Regulations."

**Why it matters:** E-bikes and motorized scooters have surged in popularity, especially among teenagers, and cities across California have moved to spell out where the devices can go and how they can be ridden. Walnut's action brings the small city into that wave — and, with a fine schedule attached, gives its rules teeth.

What the rules actually say is where the public record thins out. The specifics — exactly what conduct the ordinance prohibits, whether it addresses sidewalk riding, rider age or helmets, and how large the fines run — are not laid out in the records available to us. Those details should be confirmed in the text of Resolution 26-25 or in the April 22 meeting minutes before anyone treats a particular rule or dollar figure as settled.

The same meeting carried a second, quieter item: Resolution 26-24, which would adopt the 2026 Multi-Jurisdictional Hazard Mitigation Plan. That is less an everyday concern than a bureaucratic necessity — a FEMA-approved hazard mitigation plan is a standard prerequisite for a city to qualify for federal disaster-mitigation grants, the money that helps pay for projects that reduce damage from wildfire, flood and earthquake before disaster strikes.

A note on what this account can and can't say: the record in hand for the April 22 meeting is the agenda, which shows what the council was scheduled to act on, not how the vote fell. The e-bike and scooter regulations, though, the city has itself publicized as in place, which is why they're reported here as real.
