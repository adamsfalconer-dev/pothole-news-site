> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents June 2026 events.*

Monterey Park did not have a fire. It had the smoke.

For more than a week in June, the air over the city was governed by a blaze burning miles away in Boyle Heights, to the west — and the city answered by emptying its own summer calendar.

On June 17, a fire broke out at a Lineage cold-storage warehouse in Boyle Heights, a roughly 500,000-square-foot facility packed with an estimated 85 million pounds of frozen food and cooled by ammonia refrigerant, NPR reported. It burned for about a week before crews knocked it down around June 24, sending smoke drifting east. Locally, Monterey Park took to calling it the "Los Palos Fire."

The city measured its response in its parks-and-recreation schedule. Citing air-quality concerns, Monterey Park canceled all outdoor recreation programs through June 28 — including a June 25 watch party and farmers market and, two days later, its Independence Day celebration at Barnes Park, scheduled for Saturday, June 27. At fire stations, the city handed out N-95 masks, two per household.

**Why it matters:** Smoke does not stop at a city line, and neither did this. A community with no fire of its own still absorbed the health risk and lost a marquee public event, because the nearest big industrial blaze happened to sit upwind. It is a small, concrete lesson in how a regional hazard becomes a local one.

The air quality was real. The South Coast Air Quality Management District issued and extended a smoke advisory, and regional fine-particle pollution — the PM2.5 that lodges deep in the lungs — climbed into the "unhealthy" range after a Friday reignition. Officials did not publish a specific air-quality index reading for Monterey Park, and this account does not assert one.

The emergency reached higher offices, too. By around June 20, Los Angeles Mayor Karen Bass and Gov. Gavin Newsom had issued emergency declarations, according to CBS Los Angeles. Los Angeles County Supervisor Hilda Solis cited a smoke radius of roughly 2.5 miles reaching about 250,000 households across unincorporated East Los Angeles, where relief centers were opened.

What started the fire remains officially unsettled. NPR and other outlets reported that it is believed to have begun during rooftop solar-array testing by contractors, but investigators have not confirmed a cause, and the incident has been described as under investigation.
