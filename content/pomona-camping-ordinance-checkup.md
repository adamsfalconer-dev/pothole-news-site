Pomona's rules on camping, sleeping and storing property on public land get their first public airing since adoption on Thursday, when the Parks and Recreation Commission receives an update on Chapter 46, Article 10 of the city code.

The agenda's attachments trace the law to an April 20 city council item — the adoption staff report and the April 20 council staff report are packaged with Thursday's update, along with the ordinance text itself. That adoption predates this desk and was never covered; we're saying so rather than papering over it. What we haven't yet read: the attachments themselves. The vote count, the ordinance number, and the specific enforcement terms are not in the agenda face, and we're not asserting them until the documents are pulled.

**Why it matters:** Camping-and-storage ordinances are where a city's homelessness policy stops being a plan and becomes an instruction to police and park staff — who gets moved, whose property gets taken and stored, and under what process. A receive-and-file at the parks commission is a low-stakes venue, which is exactly what makes it useful: it's the first public window into how the April law is being used, in the rooms where it applies.

The same Thursday agenda carries a skateboarding item for Ralph Welch Park and a capital-projects update.
