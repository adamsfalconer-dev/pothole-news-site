> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive.*

Eileen Wang was the mayor of Arcadia in February. By the end of May she had resigned, pleaded guilty in federal court to acting as a secret agent of the Chinese government, and become a case number awaiting sentencing.

Wang pleaded guilty May 29 to a single count of acting within the United States as an agent of a foreign government — the People's Republic of China — without notifying the U.S. attorney general, a violation of federal law that carries up to 10 years in prison. According to prosecutors, Wang, 58, and a Chino Hills man, Yaoning "Mike" Sun, worked from late 2020 through 2022 at the direction of Chinese government officials, running a website called the "U.S. News Center" that posted content at Beijing's request. Wang admitted she never notified the attorney general of that work, as the law requires. She has been free on $25,000 bond and is scheduled to be sentenced Oct. 6.

Sun was prosecuted in a separate case. He pleaded guilty last October and was sentenced in February to four years in federal prison.

The case surfaced publicly on May 11, when prosecutors unsealed a charge that had been filed under seal in April; Wang resigned her council seat the same day. Arcadia has since filled the vacancy without waiting for the criminal case to end. On May 19 the council appointed Paul Cheng as mayor and David Fu as mayor pro tem. After interviewing seven applicants for Wang's District 3 seat, the council appointed John Han, who was sworn in June 16. He will serve until the results of the Nov. 3 city election are certified.

Wang has not spoken publicly about the case, and her attorneys have not commented on the record. The admitted conduct ran from 2020 into December 2022 — the same year Arcadia voters elected her to the council. She took her oath of office that December.
