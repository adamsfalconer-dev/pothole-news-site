> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents events of May 2026; sentencing is set for October.*

Eileen Wang was elected to the Arcadia City Council in 2022 and served a rotation as mayor. On May 29, she stood in a federal courtroom and pleaded guilty to a felony almost no local official ever faces: acting inside the United States as a secret agent of the People's Republic of China.

According to the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Central District of California, Wang, 56, admitted that from late 2020 through 2022 she and a co-defendant — Yaoning "Mike" Sun, 65, of Chino Hills — operated at the direction of PRC government officials, running a website called the "U.S. News Center" that posted pro-Beijing content on Chinese officials' instructions. She acknowledged that she never notified the U.S. Attorney General she was acting as a foreign agent, as the law requires.

**Why it matters:** This is not a distant Washington case. Two San Gabriel Valley residents — an Arcadia councilmember and a Chino Hills man — admitted in federal court to working for a foreign government while one of them held elected office in one of our cities. The question that leaves behind is a local one: what did Wang decide as a councilmember during the years she was acting for Beijing, and has Arcadia looked back at that record?

Wang was charged by criminal information on May 11 and resigned her council seat as the case became public. She pleaded guilty little more than two weeks later. Sentencing is set for October 6; the single count carries a statutory maximum of 10 years.

Her co-defendant is already serving time. Sun pleaded guilty in October 2025 and is serving a four-year sentence, the U.S. Attorney's Office said. The prosecution was announced by First Assistant U.S. Attorney Bill Essayli and the Justice Department's National Security Division, with the FBI investigating.

A word on what this story does *not* include. A July magazine investigation has since layered additional, unproven claims onto the case; because those rest partly on anonymous sourcing and no court has tested them, they are held out here. What Wang admitted under oath is serious enough on its own, and it is the part that is settled.
