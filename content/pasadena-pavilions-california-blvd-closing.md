For nearly half a century, the Pavilions at 845 East California Boulevard has been the grocery store you could walk to. On September 5, it closes.

Its parent company, Albertsons, gave two reasons, reported by Pasadena Now: the store's lease is expiring, and it is one of the "stores that do not meet financial expectations." In corporate terms, that is routine. On the ground near South Lake Avenue, it is the loss of a neighborhood fixture that has served Pasadena for about 48 years.

**Why it matters:** A grocery store within walking distance is not a small thing. For the residents nearest to 845 East California — older neighbors, people without a car, anyone used to a short walk for dinner — its closing turns an errand on foot into a drive, and shrinks the map of fresh food that is genuinely reachable. That is the quiet cost a balance sheet does not show.

The city noticed. Councilmember Jason Lyon, who represents District 7, called the news "bad news, and sad news," and said he expected the closure to mean more car trips and less convenience for the people who live around the store.

Some things are not yet known, and we will not guess at them. Albertsons did not disclose how many employees work at the store or where they might be reassigned, and no figure should be assumed.

The closure lands harder because of how long the store has been there. Nearly five decades is long enough that the same households have shopped the same aisles across generations; a closing at that age is less a business event than the end of a habit.
