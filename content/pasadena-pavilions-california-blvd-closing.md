> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents a closure announced July 2026.*

For nearly half a century, the Pavilions at 845 East California Boulevard has been the grocery store you could walk to. On September 5, it closes.

Its parent company, Albertsons, gave two reasons, reported by Pasadena Now: the store's lease is expiring, and it is one of the "stores that do not meet financial expectations." On the ground near South Lake Avenue, it is the loss of a neighborhood fixture that has served Pasadena for nearly 50 years — and a grocery store within walking distance is not a small thing. For the residents nearest to 845 East California — older neighbors, people without a car, anyone used to a short walk for dinner — its closing turns an errand on foot into a drive, and shrinks the map of fresh food that is genuinely reachable.

Councilmember Jason Lyon, who represents District 7, called the news "bad news, and sad news," and said he expected the closure to mean more car trips and less convenience for the people who live around the store.

Albertsons has not disclosed how many employees work at the store or where they might be reassigned. No state notice of the closure's employment impact had been filed as of this writing — California law requires large employers to file that notice roughly 60 days ahead of a mass layoff or closure, which for a September 5 date would fall right around now, so its absence today does not mean one won't appear in the coming weeks.
