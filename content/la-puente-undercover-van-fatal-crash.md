> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents a July 2026 collision still under investigation.*

Early on July 2, at Amar Road and Evanwood Avenue in La Puente, a motorcycle and a van collided. The motorcyclist, David Cano, 46, died at the scene. The van, according to reporting, was unmarked — driven by an undercover Alhambra police officer serving a search warrant in the area.

**Why it matters:** When a police vehicle kills someone, the public's first question is not how it happened but who decides what happened — and here that question has no clean answer. La Puente is policed by the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department, which would ordinarily investigate a fatal collision on its streets. But the vehicle belonged to a different department, Alhambra's, operating undercover in the Sheriff's territory. Two agencies, one death, and a warrant operation in the middle is exactly the arrangement that makes a transparent, conflict-free investigation both harder and more necessary.

So far, transparency has been thin. When reporters asked, Alhambra police and the Sheriff's watch commanders declined to answer questions, and no agency has said plainly which one is investigating. The county Medical Examiner identified Cano; beyond that, the public record is mostly what witnesses and reporters have assembled.

One correction worth making, because a careless headline muddied it: only Cano died. The officer was not reported hurt. There was no second fatality, whatever one headline implied.

This account does not assign fault — the cause is under investigation, and the facts of the collision itself are not established. What it flags is narrower: a man is dead after a collision with an undercover police vehicle, and the agencies involved have not said who is reviewing it. That is an answerable question, and the answer belongs to the public.
