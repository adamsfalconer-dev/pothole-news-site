> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents May–June 2026 events.*

Roughly 642,000 people passed through the gates of the 104th Los Angeles County Fair, according to the preliminary count Fairplex released as the run wrapped.

The fair, themed "Play Your Way," ran 17 days, May 7 through 31, at the Pomona fairgrounds. Fairplex put preliminary attendance at 642,081 — an average of about 38,000 guests a day. President and CEO Walter Marquez is quoted in the wrap-up.

**Why it matters:** The county fair is Fairplex's signature annual event and a marquee gathering on the Pomona calendar, the kind of run whose real size ripples out to local vendors, hotels and seasonal workers. So the actual number matters — and it lands well below what the fairgrounds had advertised. Pre-fair marketing had touted an expectation of more than 1.5 million guests; the preliminary tally came in at 642,081, less than half that.

Fairplex did not release an economic-impact or revenue figure for the 2026 run, so the fair's dollar footprint this year is undisclosed.

The wrap-up did put numbers to the fair's civic side. Its FairKids program brought 19,492 students and chaperones on field trips, and fairgoers donated 105,152 cans of food across three "Food Drive Thursdays."
