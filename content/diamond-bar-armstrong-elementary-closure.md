> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents May 2026 events.*

When the 2026–27 school year opens, Armstrong Elementary in Diamond Bar will not. On May 13, the Pomona Unified School District board voted 4–1 to close the school and consolidate its students into Golden Springs Elementary, another Diamond Bar campus, effective that year.

A school closure is among the most personal decisions a district makes. For Armstrong's children and parents, it means a new campus, new teachers and new routines next fall. The board's stated reason is enrollment: like many California districts, Pomona Unified has been educating fewer students, and it said Armstrong's enrollment has been declining for several years.

The decision is also a reminder of who runs Diamond Bar's schools. Pomona Unified is headquartered in Pomona, but its boundaries reach into Diamond Bar, where both Armstrong and Golden Springs sit. Districtwide, PUSD serves about 21,342 students across 39 schools; folding two campuses into one is the kind of move districts make when classrooms empty faster than budgets can absorb.

The vote was 4–1, with a single board member opposed. The Daily Bulletin's Allyson Vergara first reported the merger of the two Diamond Bar elementary schools.

What the vote settled is that Armstrong closes. What it left open are the questions that matter most to the people affected: what becomes of the Armstrong campus — sold, leased or repurposed — and where the school's teachers and staff will be reassigned. Those answers were not part of the decision to close.
