Pomona's Police Oversight Commission meets Tuesday with the two things a civilian watchdog most needs on one agenda: the department's pitch for FLOCK license-plate cameras, and a full year of internal-affairs case data.

The data is the quieter, bigger item. Attached to the police department's June monthly report is a running Internal Affairs case log spanning June 30, 2025 through June 30, 2026, with a legend decoding how each case was disposed. It's the first such year-spanning log we've seen on the commission's agendas — the raw material for the question oversight bodies exist to ask: what happens when someone complains about a Pomona officer?

**Why it matters:** A five-member civilian commission is only as strong as the records in front of it. Tuesday puts complaint-handling data and surveillance-technology adoption in the same room on the same night — the review function and the expansion function of modern policing, side by side.

An honesty note: the attachments — the FLOCK deck and the IA log itself — are posted but we haven't read them yet, so we're making no claims about case counts, dispositions, or what the camera system would cover and cost. We'll read both before Tuesday night.

The agenda also seats a new ad-hoc committee on "Administrative Protocols Development" (two commissioners to be appointed) and takes a report-out from the monthly-reports ad-hoc.
