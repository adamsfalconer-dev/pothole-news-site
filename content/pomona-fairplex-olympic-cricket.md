Ten weeks after the first shovel went in, Pomona had an Olympic venue. The cricket ground at Fairplex broke ground April 22, hosted its first professional match July 1, and in 2028 will stage all 28 matches of cricket's first Olympic appearance since 1900.

The stadium is the project of the Knight Riders Group, the Bollywood-backed franchise company co-owned by actor Shah Rukh Khan, which announced a partnership with Fairplex in March to build a home for its Major League Cricket team, the Los Angeles Knight Riders. The Associated Press put construction at $21 million; neither Fairplex, run by a private nonprofit, nor the Knight Riders Group, a private company, has disclosed the lease terms behind the deal, and neither is obligated to.

What the parties have shown off is the build. LandTek, the contractor, says it moved roughly 32,000 metric tons of earth and delivered the project on time; the AP describes a 200,000-square-foot facility with eight pitches — most grounds have four to six, Knight Riders chief executive Venky Mysore told the wire service — that went from groundbreaking to completion in under 70 days. Temporary stands seat about 5,000; the AP reports permanent seating for more than 20,000 is planned within the next year or so. At the April groundbreaking, Mayor Tim Sandoval stood alongside Fairplex chief executive Walter Marquez, International Cricket Council chief executive Sanjog Gupta and Niccolo Campriani of LA28, the Games' organizing committee, while Khan appeared by video. "[W]e are excited to partner with Knight Riders to present cricket, one of the world's largest sports, on our campus," Marquez said in the March announcement.

The field itself has had a good two weeks. The Knight Riders lost the ground's first match on July 1 to the Washington Freedom before a crowd the AP put at about 2,000, then won their way to second place in the league standings, clinching a playoff spot with a July 12 win over the Texas Super Kings. Seven league matches were played in Pomona through July 5; the playoffs move north, with the final set for July 18 at the Oakland Coliseum.

The Olympic stakes carry a twist. Under qualification rules the ICC announced June 29, six men's and six women's teams will compete in 2028 — and the United States, as host, is guaranteed nothing. To claim an automatic berth, a U.S. team must climb into the top 15 of the world Twenty20 rankings between June 30 and the end of December this year. Failing that, its last path to its own stadium runs through a qualifying tournament in 2027.
