Starting this month, three of Chino Hills' advisory commissions meet on new regular schedules. The city says its Parks & Recreation, Planning, and Public Works commissions revised their meeting calendars in July "to better align with commission workloads and improve efficiency."

**Why it matters:** Commission meeting times are the plumbing of local participation. The Planning Commission in particular is where development and land-use questions get their first public airing, so when its meeting night moves, so does the window for residents to weigh in — often without much notice. A schedule change is minor until it's the reason someone missed the hearing on a project down their street.

One caveat we're keeping honest: the city announced that the schedules changed, but we have not yet confirmed each commission's exact new day and time from the city calendar, so we're not publishing specific meeting dates we haven't verified.
