Ray Marquez, a sitting Chino Hills councilmember as of this writing, is now president of the Southern California Association of Governments' Regional Council for fiscal year 2026-27 — formally elected at SCAG's May General Assembly, per the organization's own newsroom.

**Why it matters:** SCAG is the body that hands every city in six counties its housing-production targets and writes the regional transportation plan those cities must build against. Cities usually experience SCAG as weather — numbers that arrive from somewhere else. For the next year, the somewhere else is chaired from Chino Hills. That doesn't let Marquez set his own city's allocation, but it puts a footprint official at the center of how the next round of regional housing and transportation decisions gets framed.

The timing is busy at home: Marquez — whom the Champion's Marianne Napoles reported in June as an early supporter of the city's proposed 1-cent sales tax — sits on the council that decides Tuesday whether that measure goes to the November ballot. Regional president Monday, local tax vote Tuesday: two very different rooms, one calendar.

Nobody in the local press localized the SCAG election when it happened in May; we're catching it up now rather than leaving it uncovered.
