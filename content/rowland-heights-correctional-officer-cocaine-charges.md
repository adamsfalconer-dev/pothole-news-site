> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents an April 2026 arraignment in a case that remains pending.*

An officer of the state prison system stood for arraignment on April 17, charged with helping move cocaine through a rented house in Rowland Heights.

According to the Los Angeles County District Attorney's Office, Jesus Reyes — a California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation officer — and a second man, Jose Luis Aldahir Rocha Luque, each face three felony counts: conspiracy to sell a controlled substance, possession for sale, and transportation between counties. Prosecutors filed the charges in March; the two were arraigned in downtown Los Angeles.

**Why it matters:** The charge that lands closest to home is not the quantity of the drugs but the badge. When the office that filed the case is the DA's Justice System Integrity Division — the unit that exists specifically to prosecute public officials and law enforcement — the story is about whether the systems meant to enforce the law policed one of their own. And the setting is ours: prosecutors say the operation ran through an ordinary house in unincorporated Rowland Heights.

The allegations, as laid out by prosecutors, are these: Luque rented the Rowland Heights home in June 2023 to use as a stash house, and on August 1 of that year Reyes allegedly drove cocaine from Mexico to it. Investigators say they seized about 20 kilograms of cocaine and nearly $200,000 in cash. The case was investigated by the Riverside County Sheriff's Office and the DEA; each defendant faces a maximum of 20 years and four months in county jail.

None of that is proven. These are allegations in a charging document, both men are presumed innocent, and the case is unresolved. This account reports what the government has publicly charged — not a conclusion about guilt.
