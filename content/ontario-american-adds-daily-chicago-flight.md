> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents July 2026 events.*

Ontario International Airport is adding a daily nonstop to Chicago. On July 1, the airport authority said American Airlines will begin once-a-day service between Ontario and Chicago O'Hare on December 17 — a direct route from the Inland Empire to one of the country's major markets.

The flights will run on a Boeing 737 with 172 seats, according to the Ontario International Airport Authority. This is an addition, not yet an operation: the service does not begin until December 17, 2026.

**Why it matters:** Air service is economic infrastructure, and every nonstop Ontario adds is one its travelers don't have to drive to Los Angeles International to catch. A daily Chicago flight gives Inland Empire residents and businesses a direct route east — and gives the airport another data point in a growth story it has been building for years.

Alan D. Wapner, president of the airport authority's governing board, cast the addition as part of a long relationship: by his account, American has served Ontario since 1970.

One caveat worth stating plainly: this is an announced route, and announced routes can shift before their first flight. As of the airport's July 1 statement, the start date is December 17.
