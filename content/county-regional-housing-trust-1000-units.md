> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents June 2026 events.*

The deal that pushed the San Gabriel Valley Regional Housing Trust past a milestone is right here in Pomona. On June 24, the trust's board approved funding for Mission Suites, a 119-unit complex — a mix of one-, two- and three-bedroom apartments — where the affordability was slated to lapse around 2030.

Rather than build something new, the trust stepped in to *preserve* what already exists: keeping the 119 units restricted to households earning no more than 60% of the area median income. The trust calls it its first preservation deal, a different play from the new construction it usually funds.

**Why it matters:** Affordable housing doesn't only disappear when it fails to get built — it disappears when the rent restrictions on existing buildings expire and the units convert to market rate. For 119 Pomona households, that clock was ticking toward 2030. Preservation deals like this one are how a place keeps affordable homes it already has, often faster and cheaper than building from scratch.

The Mission Suites approval also carried the trust past a threshold it had been climbing toward: its own announcement says it has now funded more than 1,000 affordable units. Pasadena Now, in reporting by Gab Apo, put the precise figure at 1,038 affordable units plus more than 130 interim and shelter beds.

The trust is a joint-powers authority — a government agency formed by roughly 25 San Gabriel Valley cities together with the region's council of governments, the SGVCOG — created to pool money and move faster on affordable housing than individual cities can alone. Its board chair, Emmanuel J. Estrada, is quoted in the milestone announcement.

In a separate June action, the trust awarded $4.4 million toward a 52-unit senior housing complex in South Pasadena.
