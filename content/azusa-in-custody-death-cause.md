> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents the March–May 2026 record of a death investigation.*

Eric Valencia was 37. He was arrested by Azusa police on March 20 on suspicion of DUI and child endangerment, and released three days later. On March 26, he was found dead inside an out-of-service Azusa Police Department patrol SUV parked at the station.

On May 31, the county coroner's finding became public: Valencia died of hyperthermia — overheating — and the death was ruled accidental, with no signs of major trauma.

**Why it matters:** An accidental cause does not answer the question the record leaves behind. A man ended up inside a police department's own vehicle, on police property, and was not found for days. However he got there, the facts that follow are about the department's custody of its equipment and its grounds: whether a patrol vehicle should have been unlocked, and whether anyone was checking it. Those are answerable questions, and they belong to the public.

The department has released the arrest bodycam and station surveillance, and said it retained an independent investigator. Chief Rocky Wenrick said Valencia showed "no sign of distress" when he was released. The family's attorney, Michael Carrillo, has been blunt about what he sees: the death, he said, "could have been prevented by the simple task of locking the patrol car or looking inside it," and he noted that the autopsy did not include a toxicology screen.

No one is accused of a crime here, and this account does not allege one. It documents a record with a hole in it — the gap between a man being released and a man being found — that the department has not yet fully explained.
