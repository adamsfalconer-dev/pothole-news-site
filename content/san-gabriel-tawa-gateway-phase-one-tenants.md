> *Reported July 2026 as part of our founding archive — this account documents June 2026 events.*

One of San Gabriel's biggest retail draws is reinvesting in itself. TAWA Gateway — the 12-acre center at Valley Boulevard and Del Mar Avenue that used to be Focus Plaza — unveiled the first phase of its redevelopment on June 30, naming 14 new food, entertainment and lifestyle tenants set to open between now and early 2027.

Anchoring the lineup is 99 Ranch Market, the Asian grocery chain owned by the center's developer, TAWA. Among the named tenants are Guiji Tea, the hot-pot chain Happy Lamb and Bingz Crispy Burger; another, The Best Shop, opened June 27.

**Why it matters:** Valley Boulevard is a spine of the San Gabriel Valley's Asian-American commerce, and TAWA Gateway is one of its busiest draws — the developer counts roughly 3 million visitors a year. A 14-tenant reinvestment is a sizable bet on in-person dining and retail, and for San Gabriel it means foot traffic, jobs and sales-tax dollars concentrated on one of the city's signature blocks.

The redevelopment is the work of Sunny Skies Terrace, whose chairman is Jonson Chen. The center was rebranded from Focus Plaza to TAWA Gateway in 2023; the June announcement covers Phase One only.

The developer described the site by its footprint — about 12 acres — but did not disclose the project's total square footage, so the scale of the new retail space isn't yet on the record.
